WiseTech AI Restructuring

Exploring AI-driven transformation and its impact on the workforce

AI-Driven Layoffs

WiseTech Global plans to cut up to 2,000 jobs over the next two years, citing an "AI transformation program." CEO Zubin Appoo stated, "The era of manually writing code as the core act of engineering is over".

Market Performance: The company has faced a turbulent period, with its share price dropping significantly in recent months. "They've been pretty hammered by the markets. They're down 58% over the last six months".

Redditor Reactions and Speculation

AI-Washing: Many redditors suspect that the AI narrative is a cover for lagging business, outsourcing, or offshoring. "I'm guessing this is bullshit to cover some combination of lagging business, outsourcing, and offshoring. AI-washing, in other words".

Financial Troubles: Some point to recent financial performance as the real reason for layoffs. "Their y/y revenue is down 34% last quarter. Share price down 57%. They are failing as a business and need to cut costs lol".

Impact on Developers: Concerns are rising about the future of junior developers. "My experience in the industry is that it is making the job of a junior dev fairly redundant (or fewer)".

Broader Implications of AI in the Workforce

Job Displacement: There is fear that AI will lead to more job losses across industries. "Watch as our elected officials do nothing to address the ongoing job losses that'll be created under the excuse of AI".

Productivity vs. Employment: While AI increases productivity, it may reduce the need for human labor. "It'll be a tool that enables developers to be even more productive but they don't be paid more. Because they are more productive, fewer staff are needed".

Personal Experiences with WiseTech

Interview Process: Some individuals highlight a high bar and potential ghosting. "Their bar is too high for what they pay".

Account Issues: Others report frozen accounts and poor support. "My account was closed today, October 17, after more than 3 weeks of absurd exchanges with their so-called 'compliance' team".
